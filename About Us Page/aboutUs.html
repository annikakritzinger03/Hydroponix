ABOUT THE PROJECT
The Involved Universities
Penn State
Belgium Campus

Both Penn State and Belgium Campus partake in this incredible hydroponics project, where students are given the opportunity to collaborate with international partners for a quarterly period.
This offers the following benefits for students involved in this project:

Interacting with students from another university (and another culture) provides students with the opportunity to learn about different traditions and allows students to see and understand different perspectives and opinions, which improves creativity, critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
Working with students from another university and cultures encourages collaboration and teamwork, allowing for better informed decisions to be made.
Participating in the project offers students a hands-on learning opportunity outside of class. Additionally, various soft skills and hard skills will be learned in the process of discovering more on hydroponics systems.
Students have access to the opportunity of presenting a prototype of the system to primary school students. This will allow students to come up with new and interactive ways of explaining the system to encourage participation and spark an interest of hydroponics in these students.

Project Expectations

This project (ENGR 422 for Penn State, and PRJ 381 for Belgium Campus) includes the design, fabrication, test, and presentation of a working hydroponics prototype. It ultimately aims to teach middle school students the importance of nutritional food, general hydroponics concepts, and the engineering principles of hydroponics in a practical, fun, and hands-on manner! It emphasizes the transformation of communities through education-based engineering. This can be further expanded on by considering that the project must be culturally accepted, relevant to the community, and also a long-term solution.

Project Teams

The project can be broken up into three subsystems. However, it is also important to note that while there will be three project teams, the subsystems must be able to successfully and seamlessly integrate with each other to form the larger system.
The three teams and their design domains are as follow:

Hydro team: Focuses on water flow and structures such as pipes. They are responsible for the primary physical tructure, as well as the monitoring of nutrients and water factors. They are also responsible for the development of a mobile app - download it here. (Note: App can only be downloaded on android devices)
Electro team: Focuses on electrical aspects and controls. They are responsible for technical aspects such as power constraints and sources, data handling and processing, and the WiFi/networking of the system. They are also responsible for creating and managing NodeRed flows that work with the sensor data.
Photon team: Focuses on lighting and plant health. We are responsible for lighting details, such as type, location, and light mapping, as well as all plant-health related factors, including nutrient intake and ensuring that other environmental factors are in the correct ranges. We are also responsible for the design, build, and management of this website.
