ABOUT THE SYSTEM

The Hardware

We chose to utilize PVC pipes, each capable of hosting five plants, as our preferred setup for the hydroponic system. This configuration was selected with the of providing an illustrative and comprehensive display of the system's functionality and benefits. By incorporating a pump into the design, we effectively manage water circulation, promote oxygenation within the nutrient solution, and facilitate the even distribution of essential nutrients to all plants, thus ensuring optimal growth and vitality throughout the system.

Why Spinich?

Spinach presents an excellent choice for hydroponic cultivation due to its adaptability to controlled environments and rapid growth rate. With its shallow root system and high nutrient requirements, spinach thrives in hydroponic setups where nutrient solutions can be precisely regulated. Additionally, spinach's compact size makes it well-suited for vertical farming and space-efficient hydroponic systems, allowing for high yields in limited spaces. Its nutritional density and versatility in culinary applications further enhance its appeal as a prime candidate for hydroponic cultivation.

The Software

In a hydroponic system, the integration of light, pH, and flow sensors plays a crucial role in maintaining optimal growing conditions for plants. Light sensors regulate the intensity and duration of artificial lighting, ensuring that plants receive the appropriate amount of light for photosynthesis. pH sensors monitor the acidity or alkalinity of the nutrient solution, allowing for precise adjustments to maintain the ideal pH level for nutrient uptake by the plants. Flow sensors, on the other hand, monitor the movement of water and nutrients throughout the system, enabling the adjustment of flow rates to prevent overwatering or underwatering. Together, these sensors contribute to the efficient management of the hydroponic environment, promoting healthy plant growth and maximizing yield.
